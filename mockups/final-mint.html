I'm a Senior AI/ML Engineer at Mayo Clinic, where I build enterprise AI systems that thousands of people use daily. Before this, I was the first engineering hire at Nobias Therapeutics, a startup using AI to discover drugs for kids with rare diseases.

I did my postdoc at Harvard Medical School / MGH / Broad Institute, working with Luca Pinello and Keith Joung on machine learning for genomics. Before that, I got my PhD from Iowa State University with Iddo Friedberg, where I worked on using deep learning to discover antimicrobial peptides.

I spent a long time in academia—PhD, postdoc, the whole thing—but for the last 4+ years I've been fully in industry. These days I care most about the boring stuff that makes AI actually reliable: evaluation harnesses, guardrails, schema constraints, the infrastructure that lets you deploy LLMs without breaking things.
