I'm a Senior AI/ML Engineer at Mayo Clinic, where I build enterprise AI systems on Google's Vertex AI. Before this, I was the first engineering hire at Nobias Therapeutics, a startup using AI to discover drugs for kids with rare diseases. I did a postdoc at Harvard Medical School and got my PhD from Iowa State in Computational Biology.

I spent a long time in academia—PhD, postdoc, the whole thing—but for the last 4+ years I've been fully in industry. These days I care most about the boring stuff that makes AI actually reliable: evaluation harnesses, guardrails, schema constraints, the infrastructure that lets you deploy LLMs without breaking things.

Work

2024 — present

At Mayo Clinic I'm building an enterprise AI search and conversation platform on Vertex AI (Gemini). Thousands of employees use it to query internal metadata. The interesting challenge is making LLM outputs reliable enough for healthcare—I built validation harnesses that hit 99% field-level accuracy, and structured-output checks for LLM-generated BigQuery SQL that cut manual query time by 80%.

2021 — 2024

I joined Nobias Therapeutics as the first engineering hire when it was basically just an idea. We were building AI to find drugs for kids with rare diseases. I led a small team designing cloud-native ML/LLM pipelines on AWS, shipped agent automations, retrieval systems, and fine-tuned models. Built an internal RAG stack with ColBERT retrieval integrated with GPT-4/Llama/Mistral. The startup stuff—wearing many hats, shipping fast, building practices like design reviews and testability from scratch.

2020 — 2021

Harvard Medical School / MGH / Broad Institute postdoc. Built PyTorch models on large sequence datasets to predict expression patterns. Spent time as a reviewer for NeurIPS, ICLR, and ISMB—which taught me what makes research actually useful versus just publishable.

2014 — 2019

PhD at Iowa State University in Bioinformatics/Computational Biology with Iddo Friedberg. Worked on using deep learning to find new antimicrobial peptides (bacteriocins) and predict antibiotic resistance. This was back when applying word embeddings to biological sequences was still novel.

2012 — 2014

My first real engineering job at Samsung R&D in Bangladesh. Built C++ benchmarking tools for Tizen (their TV/mobile OS) and integrated them into CI for nightly builds. Learned the fundamentals of shipping software.
